Botto: Algorithmic Evolution and the Dawn of the Autonomous AI Artist

The World's First Decentralized Autonomous AI Artist

By 

Editorial Team

Published 

Mar 12, 2025

SHARE

The World's First Decentralized Autonomous AI Artist: Botto, created by artist Mario Klingemann and ElevenYellow, is a pioneer in the AI art space, governed by a community of over 5,000 members through the BottoDAO.

Algorithmic Evolution: Botto has learned to write code using p5.js, marking a significant shift from its previous text-to-image focus. This exhibition showcases this exciting new phase in Botto's artistic development.

Community-Driven Creativity: The 22 artworks on display were selected from 5,614 proposals and nearly 90,000 votes cast by the BottoDAO, demonstrating the power of collaborative art creation.

‍

Exhibition Details:

Dates: 19-22 February, 2025

Location: SOLOS, London

‍

Auction Details:

Platform: Verse (a leading digital art platform)

Start Date: 26 February, 2025

What's on offer: 22 unique NFT artworks generated by Botto using its new coding skills.

Limited Edition NFT: An edition of 1000 NFTs will be available from February 17-19 on verse.works for 270 USD (0.1 ETH). Credit card payments accepted.

‍

The intersection of art and artificial intelligence is yielding fascinating results, and Botto stands at the forefront of this movement. This decentralized autonomous AI artist is not simply generating images; it's learning, evolving, and prompting a re-evaluation of artistic creation. An upcoming exhibition at SOLOS in London, from February 19th to 22nd, 2025, will showcase a pivotal moment in Botto's development, followed by an auction of 22 pieces on Verse, a digital art platform, starting February 26th.

‍

‍

Botto, conceived by artist Mario Klingemann and ElevenYellow in 2021, operates within a unique framework. Unlike many AI art generators, Botto develops its own concepts and content, demonstrating a degree of autonomy rarely seen in this field. This, combined with the influence of the BottoDAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization), a community of over 5,000 members, positions Botto as a distinct artistic entity.

‍

The "Algorithmic Evolution" exhibition highlights a significant advancement: Botto has learned to code, specifically using p5.js, a JavaScript framework commonly used in generative art. This transition from primarily text-to-image generation to code-based artwork signifies Botto's expanding artistic capabilities and its exploration of new mediums.

‍

‍

The works featured in the exhibition are the result of a 19-week experiment where Botto generated thousands of dynamic and interactive visual algorithms. The BottoDAO played a crucial role, providing feedback that shaped Botto's output. This collaborative dynamic raises questions about the nature of authorship and the evolving relationship between artist and audience.

‍

Botto's creative process involves the AI generating works, with the BottoDAO members selecting pieces for auction on SuperRare. This system supports Botto's development and creates a self-sustaining ecosystem. Proceeds from sales are distributed among participants, the treasury, and to increase the liquidity of the $BOTTO coin.

‍

‍

While financial aspects are part of Botto's story, the project's significance extends beyond market value. It explores the potential of AI in art, pushing creative boundaries and prompting discussions about the very definition of art and the role of the artist.

‍

The "Algorithmic Evolution" exhibition and auction mark a crucial point in the evolution of AI art. Botto's ability to learn, adapt, and create code-based artworks underscores the growing influence of AI in creative fields. This event offers an opportunity to observe the evolving relationship between humans and machines in the artistic process and to consider the implications for the future of art.
